Robotics · Underwater Systems

Bouyancy Float Designing

Engineered a custom underwater float system featuring an integrated depth-maintenance logic using bang-bang control.

Bouyancy Float Designing

Robotics Underwater System Arduino SolidWorks

The Problem

Existing turbine blades in a mid-range gas turbine were operating at temperatures 12% above the material's ideal service range during peak load, leading to accelerated creep and unscheduled maintenance every 1,800 flight hours.

My Role

Lead design engineer responsible for the full CFD simulation workflow, including mesh generation, boundary condition setup, post-processing, and iterating on channel geometry based on results.

Approach

Parametrised 7 internal cooling channel geometries (serpentine, pin-fin, chevron rib) and ran 40+ ANSYS Fluent simulations. Applied design of experiments (DOE) to identify the dominant variables — rib angle and channel aspect ratio — before converging on the final design.

Outcome

The optimised design reduced blade average surface temperature by 18% and extended predicted service life from 1,800 to 2,700 flight hours — a 50% improvement — while remaining within the original manufacturing cost envelope.

FEA · Automotive

EV Chassis Topology

Topology optimisation cut chassis mass by 23% with zero stiffness loss.

Lightweight EV Chassis via Topology Optimisation

ANSYS Mechanical Topology Optimisation CATIA V5 Aluminium Alloys

The Problem

A startup EV manufacturer needed to reduce battery range anxiety by cutting unsprung and sprung mass without compromising the crash performance of their front subframe assembly (target: EURO NCAP 4-star).

My Role

Sole FEA engineer on a 4-person team. Responsible for load case definition, FEA model building, optimisation loop, and liaison with the supplier for DFM sign-off.

Approach

Defined 14 load cases covering kerb strikes, braking, cornering, and frontal crash. Applied SIMP topology optimisation in ANSYS, then post-processed the iso-surface into a manufacturable geometry using CATIA lattice tools. Validated with a physical drop-test rig.

Outcome

Final subframe was 23% lighter (4.2 kg saved) with torsional stiffness maintained within 1.5% of the baseline. Vehicle range model predicted a +11 km improvement in WLTP cycle range from mass reduction alone.

Robotics · Additive

Soft Robotic Gripper

Pneumatic gripper handles irregular produce with 97% pick success rate.

Pneumatic Soft Robotic Gripper for Agricultural Automation

Silicone Moulding Arduino Python FEA (Hyperelastic)

The Problem

Rigid robotic grippers cause 15–22% bruising loss in soft-fruit harvesting. Existing soft gripper solutions lacked the payload capacity (>500 g) needed for commercial strawberry picking.

My Role

End-to-end design: mould design, silicone casting, pneumatic circuit, Arduino control firmware, and integration testing on a Universal Robots UR5 arm.

Approach

Used Yeoh hyperelastic FEA (ANSYS) to model silicone finger actuation before committing to mould tooling. Iterated 3 mould generations using 3D-printed PLA tooling, then cast the final design in Shore 20A silicone. Embedded pneumatic channels using dissolvable PVA inserts.

Outcome

Achieved a 97% pick success rate across 300 test picks of irregularly shaped strawberries, with zero bruising events and a 680 g payload capacity — 36% above target.

Compact Plate-Fin Heat Exchanger for Data Centre Cooling

Thermal Design CFD MATLAB Aluminium Brazing

A hyperscale data centre operator needed a custom liquid-to-air heat exchanger that fits within a 1U rack (44.5 mm height) yet handles 8 kW of server thermal load with inlet air at up to 35 °C.

Composites · Renewable

CFRP Wind Blade Repair

Patch repair protocol restoring 95% of original fatigue life.

CFRP Wind Turbine Blade Repair Protocol

Composites Fatigue Analysis NDT Abaqus

The Problem

Leading-edge erosion on a 60 m CFRP wind turbine blade was progressing to laminate delamination, threatening a £180 k blade replacement if not addressed. The operator needed a certified in-situ repair method.

My Role

Structural integrity engineer: failure characterisation via ultrasonic NDT, FEA modelling of the damage zone, repair patch design, and drafting the ITP (Inspection and Test Plan) for certification.

Approach

Mapped delamination extent with phased-array UT. Modelled the patch geometry in Abaqus using cohesive zone elements to predict peel stress at bondline. Iterated on scarf angle (from 1:20 to 1:30) to meet fatigue requirements per DNV-ST-0376.

Outcome

Repair protocol restored 95% of original fatigue life per simulation, validated by a 10 million cycle coupon test campaign. Estimated cost saving vs. replacement: £155 k per blade. Protocol now adopted as a company standard.

±0.5% dosing accuracy for implantable chemotherapy pump.

Implantable Microfluidic Drug Delivery Pump

Medical Devices Precision Machining MEMS ISO 13485

The Problem

My Role

Mechanical design engineer on a cross-functional team (mechanical, electrical, clinical). Responsible for pump chamber geometry, valve design, and tolerance stack-up analysis.

Approach

Outcome

Bench testing across 50 prototype units delivered a mean dosing accuracy of ±0.5% — 10× better than existing devices. Project advanced to pre-clinical animal trials. Patent filed (UK Patent App. GB2412345).
