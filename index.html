Buoyancy Float Design

Engineered a custom underwater float system featuring integrated depth-maintenance logic using bang-bang control.

Buoyancy Float Designing

Robotics Underwater System Arduino SolidWorks

WHAT

I designed and built an autonomous underwater buoyancy engine—a motorized ballast system that automatically alters its internal volume to precisely control its depth. Instead of constantly running heavy-drain thrusters to fight water currents and stay level, this system acts as the primary stability mechanism for the underwater robot (ROV), allowing it to maintain a precise depth while drastically optimizing battery efficiency.

My Role

Person-in-Charge (PIC) — responsible for the full engineering lifecycle from CAD concept in Fusion 360 through fabrication, electronics integration, and competition-day operations.

HOW

To bring this system from concept to a functional, deployment-ready prototype, I owned the mechanical, electrical, and software engineering loops: CAD Modeling: Using SolidWorks and Fusion 360, I designed the entire physical architecture from scratch. This meant modeling high-tolerance watertight enclosures, custom O-ring sealing grooves, and an electromechanical twist-action power mechanism to guarantee the system would completely resist flooding under intense hydrostatic underwater pressure. Custom PCB Designing: To eliminate a mess of loose wiring and minimize the electronic footprint, I developed a custom printed circuit board (PCB). This board cleanly distributed power across the hardware and directly routed an ESP32 microcontroller to my depth sensors and motor drivers. C++ & Arduino Programming: I wrote the core control software in C++ using the Arduino environment. The software ingested real-time pressure and depth sensor data, processed it through an active feedback loop, and sent immediate commands to the internal motors to adjust the engine's volume dynamically to fight currents and lock depth stability.

Outcome

System performed well across all competition runs, contributing to a 3rd-place podium finish at the MATE ROV 2026 Hong Kong Regionals. There is potential to further develop this system for real-world applications in underwater exploration and research, where efficient depth control is critical for long-duration missions.
